About Us

We are Group 19302 and we aim to

Due to its several consequences in different fields, climate change is considered a first-priority challenge worldwide. Among the main contributors to this widespread issue are human activities that result in the elevation of greenhouse gases level, such as CO2, in the atmosphere. One vital step to being able to address the problem is to better understand it, and that is why it is essential to build projects that simulate the relationship between climate change causes and impacts. Many solutions have been tried to detect small changes in climate change, such as NASA's NOAA and AIRS satellites. However, due to the high cost of these projects, they are not widely available. Our project, on the other hand, is an inexpensive way to detect the effect of CO2 level on air temperature and then soil moisture. The project's purpose is to get an approach that enables us to collect climate data, analyze it, and give a prospective solution with regard to data analysis. To build our prototype, a chemical reaction whose product is gaseous CO2 was implemented into a closed acrylic box containing a climate-detecting IIOT circuit composed of an MQ-135 Air Quality Sensor, DHT-22 Temperature Sensor, and a Resistive Soil Moisture Sensor, all connected to an ESP-32 board. This measuring system was connected to a Graphical User Interface (GUI), through which collected data could be visually represented on graphs in order to determine the relation between different variables. Moreover, the data collected by the prototype is stored in a Google Spreadsheet and can then be analyzed by the R programming language to obtain the p-value and demonstrate that there is a relationship between the variables. After correcting testing errors, the prototype showed positive results: in a 15-minute period, the CO2 concentration rose from 438 ppm to 1004 ppm, with an increase in temperature from 22.3 °C to 27.4 °C and a drop in the soil moisture from 56.39% to 54.71%.
A linear regression analysis performed in R Studio revealed the significance of the relationship between the increase in temperature and the decrease in soil moisture, with a p-value of less than 2.0 * 10-16. By the end of this project, many conclusions were drawn, such as the importance of choosing acrylic as our box’s raw material and the importance of applying some potential solutions, such as reducing CO2 levels using Direct Air Capture systems.
